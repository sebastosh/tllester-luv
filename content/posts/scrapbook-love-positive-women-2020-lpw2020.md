\[\*When I asked NYC-based artist Thiago Correia Gonçalves if he'd like to help make a [Love Positive Women](https://visualaids.org/projects/love-positive-women) event, he proposed making Bobó (shrimp and cassava stew) for Yemanjá, the goddess for fishermen who is celebrated on February 2nd in Brasil. When I asked poet Brad Walrond to participate, I'd already seen a poem 'Yemaya' in his forthcoming book [Every Where Alien](https://www.bradwalrond.com/). He offered his 1986, Yemaya and other poems. DIG Ferreira, Jesse Hawkes, Livia Alexander, George Ferraz and others helped out. George made another [batch of cloth hearts](https://luvhurts.co/texts/puppy-luv-cloth-hearts/) for attendees to the February 9th, NYC event. In addition to Bobó, Thiago made dendê or palm oil-infused candles that each guest would light upon arrival and then take home as a keepsake from the night.  Thiago let me help a little making the wax and filling the candles the night before ... and puréeing the cassava. xo todd\]
